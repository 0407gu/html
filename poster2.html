Wide-Field, Artifact-Suppressed Lensless Microscopy

Via Feature-Agnostic Autofocus and Multi-Target Separable Phase Retrieval

Ziyang Li, Xuyang Zhou, Sida Gao, Guancheng Huang, and Zhengjun Liu
School of Physics, Harbin Institute of Technology (HIT), China
HIT
Part 1: System Configuration

A portable, cost-effective lensless setup is designed for robust operation in non-darkroom scenarios, eliminating the need for complex optical lenses.

Fig. 1. Experimental setup for the lensless on-chip microscopy (LOCM) system, featuring a simple inline configuration.
  • Coherent Source: A fiber laser (λ = 532 nm) provides stable illumination, ensuring high-contrast diffraction patterns for phase retrieval.
  • Large-Area Detector: A standard CMOS sensor mounted on a 1D manual translation stage enables wide-field data acquisition without mechanical scanning.
  • Environmental Robustness: The algorithm is specifically designed to compensate for experimental imperfections such as platform vibration.
Part 2: Complex Field Correction

A "Divide-and-Conquer" strategy is employed to rectify geometric distortions and address the challenge of non-planar biological samples.

Fig. 2. Schematic of the complex field correction process, illustrating the autofocus and registration steps.

A. Feature-Agnostic Autofocus
Conventional metrics fail on sparse samples. TV-regularized Single-Shot Phase Retrieval (SSPR) is utilized to accurately determine the axial distance.

B. Segment-Dependent Registration
This step corrects for "diffraction scale magnification" (wavefront expansion) caused by varying propagation distances.

Part 3: MTS Phase Retrieval

To effectively eliminate environmental artifacts and cumulative noise, the Multi-Target Separable (MTS) algorithm is introduced.

u = p ⊙ (x + n)
Fig. 3. Flowchart of the MTS phase retrieval algorithm showing the iterative multi-objective optimization process.
  1. Physical Model: Establishes a multi-objective optimization framework that decouples the optical field.
  2. Optimization: Employs Momentum-accelerated SGD.
  3. Core Advantage: Explicit "Error Prediction" mechanism.
Part 4: Summary & Validation
Fig. 4. Experimental results demonstrating pixel super-resolution capabilities.

The proposed framework demonstrates significant performance improvements:

  • Full FOV: 28.6 mm2 (Macro-scale).
  • Imaging Depth: > 80 μm.
  • Resolution: Achieves a 775 nm half-pitch resolution.

Conclusion: A versatile and robust computational framework is presented that transforms a simple lensless setup into a high-fidelity imager.
